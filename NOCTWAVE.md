# Noctwave — Design Document
### ETHPrague 2026 Hackathon Project

> **One-liner:** A censorship-resistant publishing platform where creators own their content on Swarm, subscribers pay per second they're subscribed, and both parties can earn yield on idle funds — with no platform that can ever deplatform anyone.

---

## 1. The Problem

Substack, Patreon, and Mirror each solve part of the creator monetization problem, but all introduce a critical vulnerability: **platform dependency**.

- Substack can ban you and zero out your subscriber list overnight
- Patreon charges 8–12% and can hold your payouts
- Mirror is on-chain but has no subscription/payment layer and targets crypto insiders only
- All of them lock subscribers in — if the creator moves, subscribers don't follow automatically

Secondary problems:
- Monthly subscriptions are all-or-nothing: subscribe on the 28th, pay for the full month
- Creator earnings sit idle in a bank account earning nothing
- Readers have no portable proof they've engaged with content
- Discovery of independent creators is dominated by platform algorithms

**Noctwave solves all of these** by assembling a complete decentralized publishing stack that didn't previously exist in one place.

## 3. Core Innovations

### 3.1 Per-Second Streaming Subscriptions (user's idea)

**How it works:**

The subscriber doesn't pay a monthly fee upfront. Instead, they open a Superfluid stream directly to the creator's smart contract at a rate equivalent to the monthly price divided into per-second increments.

```
Monthly price: $10/month
Flow rate: 10 / (30 × 24 × 3600) = ~3.858 × 10⁻⁶ USDC/second
```

The stream runs continuously. The subscriber can cancel at any moment — the stream stops, the remaining balance is instantly available again. No refund requests, no proration math, no disputes. The smart contract handles it automatically.

**Why this matters vs. Beatstream/SubLink:**
Beatstream does this for music but has no content storage layer. The innovation here is that the Superfluid stream is *tied to content access* — access gates are controlled by whether your stream is active, checked on-chain at read time via the Swarm content decryption key release.

**Implementation:**
- Superfluid CFA (Constant Flow Agreement) on **Base Sepolia** (chain ID 84532)
- Creator deploys a `SubscriptionVault` contract
- Content encryption keys released only to wallets with an active stream above threshold flow rate

---

### 3.2 No-Loss Subscriptions — "Subscribe with Your Yield"

**The novel mechanic (not done before):**

Instead of streaming USDC from their wallet directly, a subscriber deposits their subscription budget into **Aave/Morpho**. The yield generated by that deposit streams to the creator. The principal is always 100% withdrawable.

```
Subscriber deposits $300 into Aave (30 months of $10/month subscription)
Aave yield rate: ~4% APY = ~$12/year = ~$1/month
Creator receives ~$1/month in yield
Subscriber can withdraw $300 anytime and lose no money
```

**Why this is powerful:**
- "I support this creator but I don't want to spend money" — solved
- Creators get sustainable, recurring income without subscribers feeling financial pain
- Perfectly aligned with ETHPrague's **Solarpunk** theme: sustainable economics, not extractive
- At scale: 1000 subscribers each depositing $300 = $300K in Aave generating $1K/month to the creator

**The tradeoff to communicate clearly:** At 4% APY, a subscriber needs to deposit ~$300 to generate $1/month for the creator. This works for long-term supporters, not casual subscribers. Both modes (direct stream or yield stream) should be available.

**Implementation:**
- `YieldSubscriptionVault` — wraps Aave's aTokens
- Superfluid stream opens on the *yield delta*, not on the principal
- Creator sees real-time yield streaming into their wallet

---

### 3.3 Creator Yield LP — Compounding Idle Earnings

**The idea (user's idea):**

Creators accumulate USDC from subscriber streams. Most of it sits idle. Noctwave offers a one-click "deploy earnings to LP" button that does two things:

**Option A — Simple yield (lower complexity, hackathon-safe):**
Creator deposits accumulated USDC into Aave/Morpho directly from the dashboard. Earns ~4–8% APY on idle earnings. One transaction to enable, auto-compounds.

**Option B — Creator liquidity token (ambitious, higher complexity):**
Creator launches a `$FEED` token (lightweight ERC-20) paired with USDC in a Uniswap v4 pool. Readers who want to speculate on the creator's growth buy `$FEED`. The LP earns trading fees on top of subscriber income. If the creator grows in popularity, token price rises. This is essentially a "creator bond" — early subscribers get upside.

**For the hackathon: build Option A, mention Option B as roadmap.**

**Implementation:**
- Option A: Aave supply via `IPool.supply()` — trivial
- Option B: Uniswap v4 `PoolManager`, `PositionManager` — significant complexity

### 3.4 Content Commitment & Escrow (anti-Patreon-abandonment)

**The problem:** Creators on Patreon routinely take monthly payments and disappear. Subscribers have no recourse.

**The mechanic:**
When setting up a subscription tier, the creator optionally commits to a **publishing schedule** (e.g., "4 posts per month"). Subscriber streams flow into an **escrow contract** rather than directly to the creator. The contract releases funds to the creator only after a valid Swarm content hash is submitted for each committed post.

```
Creator commits: 4 posts/month
Week 1: publishes post → contract releases 25% of monthly stream to creator
Week 2: publishes post → releases another 25%
Week 3: goes silent → no release, funds stay in escrow
End of month: unclaimed escrow auto-refunded to subscribers proportionally
```

**Why this matters:** It creates credibility for new creators. "I commit to 4 posts/month and my subscribers only pay if I deliver" is a stronger value proposition than Substack.

**Implementation:**
- `CommitmentVault` contract holds Superfluid stream escrow
- Creator submits Swarm hash as proof of publication
- Verifier checks hash is a valid Swarm CID (can be done trustlessly since Swarm content addressing is deterministic)
- Release function callable once per committed post

### 3.5 Privacy-Preserving Subscriptions

**The problem:** On a transparent blockchain, everyone can see who is subscribed to what. Subscribing to a whistleblowing newsletter, a health content creator, or a political journalist reveals your interests publicly.

**The mechanic (inspired by SubLink at ETH Cannes 2026, but applied here):**
Use **ENS stealth addresses** (EIP-5564) so that each subscription payment comes from an ephemeral address unlinked to the subscriber's main identity. The creator receives payment but cannot trace which ENS name paid them. The subscriber holds a ZK proof of active subscription locally to unlock content.

**What the creator sees:** "You have 847 subscribers." Not who they are.
**What the subscriber proves:** "I have an active subscription" — via a ZK proof generated client-side, never sent to anyone.

**Implementation:**
- EIP-5564 stealth address scheme for payment addresses
- Client-side ZK proof generation for content access (using a commitment scheme: subscriber knows the secret, contract holds the commitment)
- This is the **Best Privacy by Design** bounty's core mechanic

---

### 3.6 Portable Subscriber List & Cross-Platform Identity

**The problem:** If you leave Substack, you lose your subscriber list. They own it, not you.

**The mechanic:**
Every subscriber is an ENS name. The creator's subscriber list is literally a mapping of ENS names to stream states, stored on Swarm. If the creator moves to a different front-end or builds their own, they point their ENS to the new endpoint and all subscribers follow automatically — their stream contracts don't change.

The creator's profile lives at `alice.noctwave.eth`. ENS text records store:
- `description`: bio
- `url`: their Swarm content feed root
- `com.noctwave.subscribers`: count (aggregated, not deanonymized)
- `com.noctwave.tier.basic`: price in USDC/month

This means any app reading ENS can discover and display the creator. No API needed.

**Implementation:**
- ENS subname registry contract: `noctwave.eth` issues subnames to creators
- Swarm Feed used as the mutable content root (updatable pointer per Swarm's feed spec)
- Subscriber list persisted on Swarm as encrypted JSON

---

### 3.7 Content Discovery via Apify

**The problem:** Without an algorithm, how do readers find new creators?

**The mechanic:**
An Apify actor crawls creator ENS profiles and Swarm content roots periodically. It indexes public post titles, tags, and metadata (not paywalled content). The index is exposed via an X402-gated search API — readers pay micro-amounts per search query (or it's free for low volume). This is exactly what Apify's bounty is asking for.

The Apify actor:
1. Reads all `*.noctwave.eth` ENS subnames
2. Fetches their Swarm feed root
3. Reads public post metadata (title, excerpt, tags)
4. Builds a searchable index
5. Exposes it via an API with X402 payment for bulk/programmatic access

AI agents can also query this API to subscribe readers autonomously (an agent managing your reading list).

**Implementation:**
- Apify actor (TypeScript) with ENS → Swarm crawl logic
- X402 middleware for the search API endpoint
- This directly satisfies the **Apify bounty** ($1,700)

### 3.8 Content Encryption & Key Management

*Architecture confirmed with Swarm mentor (Áron Soós / Vojtech Simetka) at ETHPrague 2026.*

#### The problem with the naive approach

The original intuition was to give subscribers a Bee node to fetch and decrypt content from Swarm. This is too high a bar — running a Bee node requires technical setup and is a non-starter for mainstream users.

The mentor's suggestion: **separate content storage from key delivery**. Content lives on Swarm as an encrypted blob anyone can download. The decryption key lives on Swarm too — but at a location only the subscriber can find, encrypted so only they can read it.

No Bee node needed for end users. Everything goes through the Swarm **HTTP gateway** (`https://gateway.ethswarm.org`) — plain HTTPS, same as any CDN.

---

#### Two-layer storage

```
Swarm
├── /content/{postCID}
│     Encrypted blob (AES-256-GCM)
│     Publicly downloadable by anyone — useless without key K
│
└── /keys/{lookupAddress}
      encrypt(K, sharedSecret)
      Only the subscriber can compute sharedSecret
```

The lookup address is **deterministic** — derived from information both parties already have:

```
lookupAddress = keccak256(creatorAddress + subscriberAddress + postCID)
```

Creator knows both addresses and the post CID, so they know where to store the key. Subscriber knows both addresses and the post CID, so they know where to fetch it. No database, no API call, no server lookup — pure math on both sides.

The ENS subname structure reinforces this: `alice.noctwave.eth` resolves to the creator's wallet address, so the subscriber can always compute `keccak256(resolve("alice.noctwave.eth") + theirAddress + postCID)` entirely client-side.

---

#### Key derivation — wallet-agnostic ECDH

The decryption key for each subscriber is derived using **ECDH** (Elliptic Curve Diffie-Hellman) — a standard that works with any Ethereum keypair, regardless of which wallet holds it.

```
sharedSecret = keccak256(creatorPrivKey × subscriberPubKey)
             = keccak256(subscriberPrivKey × creatorPubKey)
```

Both sides compute the same secret independently. Neither needs to transmit it.

In practice, the subscriber never directly exposes their private key. Instead they sign a deterministic message — a standard `eth_sign` call supported by every Ethereum wallet:

```
message  = "noctwave-decrypt:{creatorAddress}:{postCID}"
signature = wallet.signMessage(message)          // one click, any wallet
sharedSecret = keccak256(signature)
```

The signature is deterministic (same inputs → same output for a given wallet) and impossible to forge without the private key. It serves as the entropy to decrypt K.

---

#### Wallet-agnostic design

The entire flow uses only **`eth_sign` / `personal_sign`** (EIP-191) — the most universally supported Ethereum signing primitive. No wallet-specific APIs:

| What we do NOT use | Why avoided |
|---|---|
| MetaMask `eth_decrypt` | Deprecated and removed; MetaMask-only |
| MetaMask `eth_getEncryptionPublicKey` | Also deprecated; MetaMask-only |
| WalletConnect session-specific APIs | Breaks with other connectors |

The wallet connection layer is **RainbowKit + Wagmi**. RainbowKit gives users a clean connection modal supporting MetaMask, WalletConnect, Coinbase Wallet, Rainbow, Ledger, and any injected provider. Wagmi provides the React hooks (`useSignMessage`, `useAccount`, `usePublicClient`) that work identically regardless of which wallet is connected.

```typescript
// Works with any wallet — MetaMask, Rainbow, Coinbase, Ledger, etc.
const { signMessageAsync } = useSignMessage()

const decryptContent = async (creatorAddress, postCID, encryptedContent) => {
  const message = `noctwave-decrypt:${creatorAddress}:${postCID}`
  const signature = await signMessageAsync({ message })
  const sharedSecret = keccak256(signature)
  return aesDecrypt(encryptedContent, sharedSecret)
}
```

One `signMessageAsync` call. One wallet prompt. Works everywhere.

---

#### Full publish / subscribe / read flow

**When a creator publishes a post:**
```
1. Generate random AES-256-GCM key K
2. Encrypt post content with K → upload to Swarm → get postCID
3. For each current subscriber S:
     lookupAddr = keccak256(creatorAddr + S.address + postCID)
     encryptedK = ECDH_encrypt(K, sharedSecret(creator, S))
     upload encryptedK to Swarm at lookupAddr
4. Update content Feed (append postCID to table of contents)
5. Update ENS text record → new Swarm Feed root
```

**When a new subscriber opens a Superfluid stream:**
```
1. Smart contract emits FlowUpdated event
2. Creator's key service detects new subscriber S
3. For ALL existing posts:
     lookupAddr = keccak256(creatorAddr + S.address + postCID)
     encryptedK = ECDH_encrypt(K_post, sharedSecret(creator, S))
     upload to Swarm at lookupAddr
4. XMTP message to S: "Your access to alice.noctwave.eth is ready"
```

**When a subscriber reads a post:**
```
1. Fetch encrypted content from Swarm gateway (plain HTTPS, no Bee node)
2. Compute lookupAddr = keccak256(creatorAddr + myAddr + postCID)
3. Fetch encryptedK from Swarm gateway at lookupAddr
4. Sign deterministic message → derive sharedSecret (one wallet prompt)
5. Decrypt K from encryptedK using sharedSecret
6. Decrypt content with K → render in browser
```

**When a subscriber cancels (stream closes):**
```
No action needed on existing content — keys already delivered.
Creator simply stops uploading new keys for that address going forward.
Past content remains accessible (subscriber paid for it).
Future content: new keys not generated for cancelled address.
```

---

#### Native Swarm ACT — confirmed not used

Swarm has a built-in feature called **ACT (Access Control Trie)** that implements a similar pattern at the protocol level. We confirmed with the Swarm mentor at ETHPrague 2026: ACT works well when the subscriber runs a local Bee node, but is not straightforward to use via the public HTTP gateway.

Since we are not requiring end users to run a Bee node, **ACT is out**. The custom ECDH approach described above is the confirmed implementation path.

---

#### What this adds to the Swarm pitch

This architecture is directly aligned with the Swarm **Verified Fetch** bounty ($250) — the pattern of fetching data from a Swarm gateway and verifying its integrity client-side is exactly what that bounty describes. The subscriber fetches the encrypted key, verifies the Swarm content hash matches the expected CID, then decrypts. Trustless, gateway-based, no node required.

## 4. Multimedia Content — Video, Images & Audio

Noctwave is not a text-only platform. Creators publish in whatever format fits their work. All media types are treated as first-class content, stored on Swarm, and encrypted for paid tiers using the same key-release mechanism as text posts.

### Supported content types

| Type | Storage | Paid gating | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Text / Markdown | Swarm blob | Symmetric key | Core; already designed |
| Images | Swarm blob | Same key as parent post | JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP |
| Audio (podcast, music) | Swarm blob | Symmetric key | MP3, AAC, FLAC |
| On-demand video | Swarm + HLS | Per-chunk key or full-file key | See below |
| Live streaming | Livepeer → archive to Swarm | Session key | Optional / roadmap |

### On-demand video on Swarm

Swarm stores arbitrary binary data in 4KB chunks. A video file is chunked, content-addressed, and retrievable via the Swarm gateway's HTTP API — which is already an HTTP byte-range compatible endpoint. This means standard HLS playback works with minimal plumbing.

**Upload flow:**
```
Creator selects video file
  → Client transcodes to HLS (multiple quality tiers) using ffmpeg.wasm in-browser
  → Each .ts segment + master playlist uploaded to Swarm
  → Root manifest CID stored in post metadata on Swarm Feed
  → Thumbnail (single frame) uploaded separately as public preview
```

**Playback flow (subscriber):**
```
Subscriber loads post
  → Contract confirms active Superfluid stream → releases decryption key
  → Client fetches HLS manifest CID from Swarm
  → Video.js or HLS.js fetches segments from Swarm gateway
  → Decrypts each segment client-side before passing to player
```

**Why not Livepeer for on-demand?**
Livepeer is a decentralized *transcoding* network — it doesn't store content. It still needs a storage backend. Using Livepeer for transcoding + Swarm for storage is a valid combination, but adds a dependency and a bounty scope that doesn't exist here. For the hackathon, browser-side ffmpeg.wasm transcoding is simpler and keeps everything on Swarm. Livepeer stays as an optional upgrade path for live streaming.

### Live streaming (optional / roadmap)

Live streaming on Swarm is not viable — Swarm is optimised for content-addressed storage, not sub-second latency delivery. The right architecture:

```
Creator streams via OBS/browser → Livepeer ingest
  → Livepeer transcodes to HLS → served to subscribers in real-time
  → After stream ends → recording uploaded to Swarm for permanent archive
  → XMTP subscriber group notified of new VOD (see Section 5)
```

This is marked as **optional** for the hackathon. A clean demo showing on-demand video playback from Swarm is sufficient; live streaming is a roadmap feature.

### What changes in the architecture

- `CommitmentVault`: accepts Swarm CIDs of any content type — the CID is content-type agnostic
- Post metadata schema gains `content_type`, `duration_seconds` (video/audio), `thumbnail_cid`, `hls_manifest_cid`
- The Apify indexer indexes public thumbnails and post titles for discovery; it never touches paywalled content
- No new contracts needed — Swarm handles everything at the storage layer

### Prior art at ETHGlobal

- **VideoFlowOnline** (ETHOnline 2021): Livepeer + Superfluid pay-per-minute video — same concept but 5 years old, no Swarm, no content ownership
- **Switch** (ETHNewYork 2022): Livepeer + Superfluid with NFT replay drops — overlap on streaming payments
- **SafeStream** (Superhack): Livepeer + Worldcoin gating — different gating mechanism, no decentralized storage
- **CreatorCollect** (ETH Tokyo): Livepeer video gated by Lens post collects

**Differentiation:** None of the above use Swarm for permanent, censorship-resistant storage. All depend on Livepeer's network for the content itself. Noctwave's videos exist as long as Swarm exists — no platform dependency.

## 5. Decentralized Chat

Chat turns Noctwave from a one-way publishing tool into a community. A creator's subscriber group is a place subscribers talk to each other, ask the creator questions, and receive exclusive updates — all without a Discord server that can be banned or a Telegram group the creator doesn't control.

### Protocol choice: XMTP

**XMTP** is the correct choice for this integration:

| Protocol | Persistent | Group chat | ENS-native | Maturity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **XMTP** | ✅ | ✅ v3 groups | ✅ | High — used in 20+ ETHGlobal projects |
| Waku | ❌ ephemeral | ✅ | Partial | Medium |
| Swarm PSS | ✅ | ❌ limited | ❌ | Low |
| Push Protocol | ✅ | ✅ | Partial | Medium |

XMTP v3 (released 2024) supports **on-chain-gated group chats** where membership is managed programmatically. ENS names resolve to XMTP inboxes natively. Messages persist and are end-to-end encrypted. This is exactly what's needed.

**Waku** is kept as a secondary option for ephemeral real-time chat during live streams — it has sub-second latency and no storage cost. If live streaming ships, Waku handles the live chat sidebar while XMTP handles the persistent subscriber group.

### Stream-gated subscriber group

The core mechanic: **your Superfluid stream state IS your group membership**.

```
Subscriber opens stream → event listener adds wallet to XMTP group
Subscriber cancels stream → listener removes wallet from XMTP group

This runs as a lightweight off-chain service (or onchain hook via Superfluid's callback)
```

The creator never manually manages their community. The payment contract manages the guestlist.

**XMTP group structure per creator:**
```
alice.noctwave.eth
  ├── #announcements  (creator-only write, all subscribers read)
  ├── #community      (all subscribers read + write)
  └── #direct         (1:1 DMs between creator and each subscriber)
```

### Tip-with-message

A subscriber can send a USDC tip to the creator bundled with an XMTP message — one action, two outcomes. Think Ko-fi or Twitch bits, but the creator receives it in their wallet immediately and the message is end-to-end encrypted.

```
Subscriber clicks "Super Message"
  → Inputs tip amount (e.g., $2) + message text
  → One transaction: Superfluid one-time transfer + XMTP message sent simultaneously
  → Creator sees highlighted message in their XMTP inbox
  → Optionally: tipped messages shown in public feed as social proof
```

### Creator-to-subscriber broadcast

Beyond group chat, the creator can send an encrypted broadcast to all subscribers — like an exclusive newsletter update that doesn't appear on the public Swarm feed. This is the XMTP #announcements channel: creator writes once, all active subscribers receive it encrypted.

This is important for creators who want to send time-sensitive updates ("dropping new video in 1 hour") without publishing a full post.

### Privacy note

XMTP messages are end-to-end encrypted between wallet addresses. The XMTP network nodes cannot read message content. Combined with stealth address subscriptions (Section 3.5), a subscriber can participate in a creator's community without their ENS identity being linkable to their subscription payment address. These are two different keys — the chat identity and the payment identity — connected only in the subscriber's local client.

### Implementation

The XMTP JavaScript SDK (v3) handles all of this:

```typescript
// Create subscriber group on creator setup
const group = await client.conversations.newGroup([], {
  groupName: `${creatorENS} subscribers`,
  permissions: GroupPermissions.AdminOnly  // creator manages membership
})

// Add subscriber when stream opens (Superfluid event listener)
superfluidContract.on('FlowUpdated', async (sender, receiver, flowRate) => {
  if (receiver === creatorVault && flowRate > 0) {
    await group.addMembers([sender])
  } else if (receiver === creatorVault && flowRate === 0) {
    await group.removeMembers([sender])
  }
})
```

The group membership sync is an off-chain Node.js listener — no new contracts needed.

### Hackathon scope

**Build:** XMTP group creation on creator setup, stream-gated membership sync, basic #community chat UI in the creator page, 1:1 DM from subscriber to creator.

**Cut if time-pressed:** Tip-with-message (mention as roadmap), #announcements channel (merge into #community), live Waku chat.

### Prior art

- **neetchat** (ETH Buenos Aires 2026): XMTP wallet-to-wallet chat with ENS resolution — very close but no subscription gating
- **RenSplit** (ETH Buenos Aires 2026): XMTP group notifications for rent events — same pattern of contract events → XMTP messages
- **Chitty Chat** (HackFS 2023): XMTP chat token-gated via Superfluid stream — *most similar prior art*

**Key difference from Chitty Chat:** Chitty Chat gated access to a ChatGPT bot. Noctwave gates access to the creator's community and uses dynamic membership — join/leave based on real-time stream state, not a static allowlist.

## 11. Optional: AI Agent Integrations

AI agents slot naturally into Noctwave because the core stack is already agent-friendly: ENS names are machine-readable identities, Superfluid streams are programmable money, and the Apify search API accepts X402 micropayments. Agents can be first-class participants — not bolted on.

Below are four agent designs, ordered from most to least feasible in a hackathon.

---

### Agent 1: Reader Agent — Autonomous Subscription Manager ⭐ *Most feasible*

**The pitch:** You give an agent a monthly budget and a list of interests. It autonomously finds creators, opens Superfluid streams to subscribe, monitors your engagement, and cancels subscriptions you're not reading — keeping you within budget automatically.

**How it works:**

```
User sets policy:
  - monthly_budget: $15 USDC
  - interests: ["Ethereum research", "DeFi", "privacy tech"]
  - min_engagement_ratio: 0.5  (cancel if reading <50% of posts)

Agent loop (runs daily):
  1. Query Apify search API (X402 micropayment per query) for creators matching interests
  2. For each candidate: fetch recent post metadata from Swarm, score relevance with LLM
  3. Subscribe to top matches by opening Superfluid streams (spend permission check)
  4. Track which posts the user actually reads (client-side signal)
  5. Cancel streams for creators below engagement threshold
  6. Rebalance if over budget: pause lowest-scoring stream
```

**The spend permission model:**
The agent is granted a **ERC-7715 spend permission** (or a simpler smart account delegation) with hard limits: max flow rate per stream, max total active streams, daily spending ceiling. The user can pause or revoke in one transaction at any time. This is the same pattern as **Agent Paymaster** (HackMoney 2026), applied to subscription management instead of trading.

**ENS identity:**
The agent gets its own ENS subname: `reader.alice.noctwave.eth`. Its ENS text records store:
- `com.noctwave.agent.budget`: $15/month
- `com.noctwave.agent.interests`: "Ethereum research, DeFi"
- `com.noctwave.agent.type`: "reader"

Creators can see that an *agent* subscribed to them, not just a human. This is directly what the **ENS AI Agents bounty** is asking for.

**Bounties touched:**
- **ENS Bounty 1** (Best ENS Integration for AI Agents, $2K): agent has ENS subname, stores policy in text records, discovers creators by resolving ENS names
- **Apify bounty** ($1.7K): agent pays per query to the Apify content index via X402
- **Umia** ($2K): "an AI reading assistant that never overspends" is a clear venture — charge a 1% management fee on subscriptions managed

**Hackathon scope:** The agent can be a simple Node.js/TypeScript script running off-chain, calling Claude API for content relevance scoring, and using Viem to manage Superfluid streams. No on-chain agent runtime needed. Demo: show the agent subscribing to 3 creators within a $5 budget, then cancelling one after detecting low engagement.

### Agent 2: Creator Publishing Agent — Never Miss Your Commitment

**The pitch:** Creator sets a brief ("write weekly about Ethereum L2 research"). The agent drafts posts using Claude, notifies the creator for review, and on approval publishes to Swarm and submits the Swarm CID to the CommitmentVault — so the escrow releases on time.

**How it works:**

```
Creator config (stored in ENS text records):
  - schedule: "every Monday"
  - brief: "Ethereum L2 research, 600-900 words, technical audience"
  - tone: "analytical, no hype"

Agent flow:
  1. On schedule trigger: fetch recent relevant content via Apify (Ethereum research blogs, papers)
  2. Generate draft post with Claude, following creator's brief and tone
  3. Send draft to creator via notification (email or push)
  4. Creator reviews → approves / edits / rejects
  5. On approval: encrypt post → upload to Swarm → get CID
  6. Submit CID to CommitmentVault → escrow releases to creator
  7. Update Swarm Feed root → ENS text record updated
```

**Why this matters:** The CommitmentVault (Section 3.4) creates real pressure on creators to publish on schedule. This agent removes that pressure while keeping the accountability guarantee for subscribers. "Your creator never misses a post" becomes a platform property, not a personality trait.

**Bounties touched:**
- **ENS Bounty 1** ($2K): agent has ENS subname, reads brief from text records, updates content pointer post-publish
- **Apify bounty** ($1.7K): agent uses Apify to gather source material for post generation via X402
- **Swarm**: agent is the entity actually uploading to Swarm and managing Feed updates
- **Umia** ($2K): "AI ghostwriter that keeps your subscription escrow safe" — clear venture path (charge % of escrow unlocked)

**Hackathon scope:** Simpler than it sounds. The scheduling logic is off-chain (a cron job). The hard parts — Swarm upload and CommitmentVault CID submission — are already being built for the core product. The agent just adds the Claude API call and a notification step. Demo: show agent drafting a post, creator approves in the UI, post appears on Swarm and escrow unlocks.

### Agent 3: Yield Optimizer Agent — Maximize Creator APY

**The pitch:** Creator earnings accumulate as USDC. An agent monitors yield rates across Aave, Morpho, and Compound in real-time and rebalances the creator's LP between protocols to always chase the best APY — within creator-defined risk limits.

**How it works:**

```
Creator sets policy:
  - max_single_protocol: 80%   (diversification floor)
  - min_apy_delta_to_rebalance: 0.5%  (only move if gain > 0.5% APY)
  - allowed_protocols: [Aave, Morpho]

Agent loop (runs hourly):
  1. Fetch current APY from each protocol via on-chain rate calls
  2. Compare to current allocation
  3. If rebalance criteria met: withdraw from lower-yield, deposit to higher-yield
  4. Log rebalance event with before/after APY
```

**Why this is useful:** A creator earning $1,000/month in subscriptions who keeps it all in Aave at 3.5% when Morpho offers 5.2% is leaving $17/month on the table. At $10K/month subscriber income, the optimizer pays for itself many times over.

**Bounties touched:**
- **ENS Bounty 1** ($2K): agent has ENS subname `optimizer.alice.noctwave.eth`, stores policy in text records
- **Umia** ($2K): yield optimization as a managed service is a clear venture (charge basis points on yield generated)

**Hackathon scope:** Medium complexity. The DeFi interaction (Aave/Morpho supply/withdraw) is straightforward. The agent logic is a simple comparison loop. The ENS identity and text record updates are reused from the core stack. The main risk is protocol rate API availability on testnet.

### Agent 4: Sponsor Matching Agent — Decentralized Ad Network

**The pitch:** Sponsors deposit USDC with targeting criteria ("reach Ethereum developers interested in ZK proofs, budget $500"). An agent profiles creator content via Apify, matches sponsors to creators, proposes deals, and executes payment on acceptance — with no intermediary taking 30%.

**How it works:**

```
Sponsor deposits:
  SponsorPool.deposit($500, criteria={topics: ["ZK", "Ethereum"], min_subscribers: 100})

Agent flow (runs on new creator posts):
  1. New post published to Swarm → event emitted
  2. Agent fetches post content via Apify
  3. LLM scores relevance against all active sponsor criteria
  4. If score > threshold: propose deal to creator via ENS notification record
     e.g., "Include a 2-sentence mention of ZKSync in your next post for $40"
  5. Creator accepts/rejects in dashboard
  6. On accept: agent releases $40 from SponsorPool to creator wallet
  7. Creator publishes post with mention → Swarm CID logged as proof of delivery
```

**Why this matters:** The creator monetization layer today is either "Substack ads" (opaque, platform takes cut) or "direct DMs from sponsors" (manual, no escrow). A trustless sponsor pool with automated matching and proof-of-delivery is genuinely novel — and a stronger venture than the others.

**Bounties touched:**
- **Apify bounty** ($1.7K): agent uses Apify to analyze content for sponsor matching via X402
- **ENS Bounty 1** ($2K): sponsor pool and creator each have ENS names; deal negotiation via ENS text records
- **Umia** ($2K): decentralized ad network is the clearest venture of all four agents — take 5% of matched sponsorship volume
- **Network Economy track**: this is literally an on-chain economic primitive for creator monetization

**Hackathon scope:** Most complex of the four. The SponsorPool contract is new (beyond the core scope). The LLM matching logic requires a working Apify pipeline. Recommend treating this as a stretch goal or demo concept unless time is ample.

## 15. MVP

The MVP needs to demonstrate the core value proposition end-to-end — creator owns content, subscriber pays per second, cancels anytime — while hitting enough bounties to be competitive. Everything else is roadmap.

---

### Must-have (core demo, non-negotiable)

**1. Wallet connection — RainbowKit + Wagmi**
Any wallet works. No MetaMask-specific APIs anywhere. This is the entry point for both creator and subscriber.

**2. Creator onboarding**
- Claim an ENS subname (`alice.noctwave.eth`) via the `NoctwaveRegistry` contract
- Set a subscription price (USDC/month)
- Initialize a Superfluid `SubscriptionVault`

**3. Content publishing — video (primary), text + image (if straightforward)**
- Creator uploads a video → ffmpeg.wasm transcodes to HLS in-browser → segments uploaded to Swarm → manifest CID stored in post metadata
- Video.js player fetches segments from Swarm gateway, decrypts client-side
- Text posts and images use the same encryption model and can be added with minimal extra work — include them if the upload flow is already in place, skip if they would slow things down
- Public preview (title + thumbnail) visible without subscribing; full content paywalled

**4. Content encryption — ECDH, wallet-agnostic**
- Post content encrypted with AES-256-GCM key K, uploaded to Swarm
- Encrypted key stored at deterministic address `keccak256(creatorAddr + subscriberAddr + postCID)` on Swarm
- On stream open: key service generates encrypted key entry for the new subscriber across all existing posts
- Subscriber decrypts with a single `signMessageAsync` call (one wallet prompt, any wallet, no Bee node)

**5. Privacy — stealth address subscriptions (EIP-5564)**
- Subscriber pays via a stealth address derived from the creator's ENS stealth meta-address
- Creator receives payment but cannot link it to any ENS identity or wallet
- Subscriber holds a ZK proof of active subscription locally to gate content access
- Every subscription payment comes from a fresh ephemeral address — on-chain, payments are unlinkable to the subscriber's main wallet
- Touches: Best Privacy by Design bounty, ENS creative bounty (stealth addresses mentioned explicitly in their bounty description)

**6. Per-second streaming subscription**
- Subscriber opens a Superfluid stream to the creator's vault
- Flow rate = subscription price ÷ seconds in a month
- Real-time balance counter visible in the UI — the single most visceral judge moment
- On stream open: key delivery triggered for subscriber

**7. Cancel anytime**
- Subscriber closes stream → Superfluid stops flow instantly
- No new keys generated for future posts
- Already-unlocked posts remain readable (subscriber paid for them)
- Pro-rated: paid only for exact seconds subscribed
